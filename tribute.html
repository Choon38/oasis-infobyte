Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, a symbol of peace and nonviolence, led India's struggle for independence through his principles of truth, love, and compassion. His life continues to inspire millions around the world.

Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, Gandhi's legacy extends far beyond his lifetime. He advocated for social justice, equality, and the upliftment of the marginalized.

Gandhi's philosophy of Satyagraha, or nonviolent resistance, inspired movements for civil rights and freedom worldwide. His words and actions remain relevant in the pursuit of justice and harmony.

Let us remember Mahatma Gandhi's teachings and strive to build a world guided by truth, love, and nonviolence.
